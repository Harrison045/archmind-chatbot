SECTION 2 — SERVICES WE OFFER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Architectural Design
Full architectural design from concept to completion. Includes feasibility
studies, concept design, schematic design, design development, and construction
documentation. Typical project sizes range from 120 m² private residences to
18,000 m² mixed-use developments.

2.2 Sustainability & Building Performance
EDGE certification advisory (we are EDGE Experts), passive design strategy,
daylight and thermal comfort modelling, operational energy estimates, and
embodied-carbon screening. We do NOT issue energy compliance certificates — that
is done by an accredited third-party assessor; we prepare the documentation.

2.3 Planning & Permitting Support
We assist clients in assembling building-permit submissions to the relevant
Metropolitan/Municipal/District Assembly (MMDA). We coordinate the drawings; the
client is the applicant of record. Permit approval timelines are outside our
control and typically take 4–12 weeks depending on the Assembly.

2.4 Interior Design & FF&E
Space planning, material and finish specification, furniture/fixtures/equipment
schedules. Often bundled with architectural commissions but available standalone.

2.5 Construction-Stage Services
Periodic site inspection, contractor query response, shop-drawing review, and
progress certification. NOTE: Meridian provides design-intent review only. We are
NOT the contractor and do NOT supervise means/methods or site safety — that is
the contractor's legal responsibility.

2.6 [DEPRECATED] Quantity Surveying
We used to offer in-house QS. As of January 2022 this is outsourced to our
partner firm Adansi Cost Consult. Do not quote QS as an in-house service.


SECTION 3 — HOW WE DELIVER A PROJECT (WORK STAGES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We loosely follow the RIBA Plan of Work, adapted for Ghana. Our internal stage
names and what each delivers:

Stage 0 — Strategic Definition: client brief intake, site visit, project goals.
Stage 1 — Preparation & Brief: feasibility, site analysis, preliminary budget.
Stage 2 — Concept Design: massing options, 2–3 concept directions presented.
Stage 3 — Spatial Coordination: developed design, structural/MEP coordination.
Stage 4 — Technical Design: full construction documentation, specifications.
Stage 5 — Construction: site services, RFIs, certifications (see 2.5).
Stage 6 — Handover: snagging, as-built records, O&M manuals.
Stage 7 — In Use: post-occupancy review (optional, separately commissioned).

Typical durations (design stages only, excluding permitting and construction):
- Small residential (<300 m²): 8–14 weeks for Stages 1–4.
- Medium commercial (300–3,000 m²): 16–28 weeks for Stages 1–4.
- Large/mixed-use (>3,000 m²): 30+ weeks, phased.
These are indicative and depend on client decision turnaround.

SECTION 4 — FEES & PAYMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Our fees are quoted per project after a brief. As a rough guide (these are
INDICATIVE PERCENTAGE RANGES of construction cost, not fixed quotes):

| Project type            | Fee range (% of construction cost) |
|-------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Private residential     | 7% – 12%                            |
| Commercial / office     | 6% – 9%                             |
| Institutional / public  | 6% – 10%                            |
| Mixed-use (large)       | 5% – 8%                             |
| Interiors only          | 8% – 14%                            |
| Sustainability advisory | Fixed fee, quoted per scope         |

Payment is staged against work stages. Standard schedule:
- 15% on appointment (Stage 1)
- 20% at end of Concept Design (Stage 2)
- 25% at end of Spatial Coordination (Stage 3)
- 25% at end of Technical Design (Stage 4)
- 15% across Construction & Handover (Stages 5–6)

Invoices are due within 21 days. Late payment beyond 30 days may pause work.
We accept bank transfer and mobile money (MTN MoMo). We do NOT accept cash for
amounts above GHS 2,000. All fees are quoted exclusive of 21.9% VAT/levies unless
stated. Reimbursable expenses (printing, travel outside Greater Accra, statutory
fees) are billed at cost.

Deposit/retainer for new clients without a prior relationship: 15% non-refundable
on appointment. Cancellation: client pays for work completed to date plus 10% of
remaining fee.


SECTION 5 — POLICIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Sustainability Policy
Every Meridian project is screened for passive design opportunity before any
mechanical cooling is specified. Default targets: maximise cross-ventilation,
shade all west/east glazing, light-coloured roofs, and rainwater harvesting where
feasible. We aim for EDGE-compliant (≥20% savings) on all new-build commissions
where the client's budget allows.

5.2 Data & Confidentiality
Client drawings, briefs, and site information are confidential. Staff must not
share project files outside the firm without a signed NDA. Client data is retained
for 7 years after project completion, then archived or securely deleted on request.

5.3 Site Safety (Important Boundary)
Meridian staff visiting site must wear PPE (hard hat, hi-vis, boots). HOWEVER,
Meridian does not hold site-safety responsibility — the principal contractor is
legally responsible for site safety, means, and methods under the contract.

5.4 Professional Boundaries (READ CAREFULLY)
Meridian provides architectural design and advisory services. We DO NOT:
- Issue final structural sizing, foundation, or reinforcement designs (a licensed
  structural engineer of record does this and stamps it).
- Provide stamped code-compliance sign-off or fire/egress certification.
- Act as the contractor or guarantee construction cost or programme.
Any preliminary sizing or figure we give is indicative and must be verified by the
relevant licensed professional before construction.

SECTION 6 — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (CLIENTS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q: How much will my project cost?
A: We can't give an accurate figure until we understand the brief and site. As a
   very rough early guide, mid-spec construction in Accra ranges from about
   GHS 4,500 to GHS 9,000 per m² (2024, indicative, excludes land and fees).
   This varies enormously with finishes, site, and market prices — treat it only
   as an order-of-magnitude starting point and confirm with a current QS estimate.

Q: Do you handle the building permit for me?
A: We prepare and coordinate the permit drawings and documentation, but the client
   is the applicant of record and pays statutory fees. Approval timelines are set
   by the Assembly, not by us (typically 4–12 weeks).

Q: Can you work outside Accra and Kumasi?
A: Yes. We work nationwide and have done projects in Takoradi, Tamale, and Ho.
   Travel outside Greater Accra/Ashanti is billed as a reimbursable expense.

Q: How long before I see designs?
A: For a typical residence, expect first concept options about 3–4 weeks after the
   signed appointment and a completed brief.

Q: Do you do 3D visualisations?
A: Yes, photorealistic renders and walkthroughs are available. Basic massing 3D is
   included in Concept Design; high-end renders are an add-on service.

Q: Can you guarantee my project stays on budget?
A: No firm can honestly guarantee construction cost — material prices and
   contractor pricing move. We design to a target budget and flag risks early, and
   our QS partner provides cost estimates, but the final cost is set by the market
   and the contractor.

Q: What climate data do you design for?
A: Coastal projects: warm-humid tropical. Northern projects: hot-dry. We always
   confirm the actual site location and microclimate before finalising strategy.

- Ghana Standards Authority material standards (various).

  amendments. Never treat a code figure as universal across jurisdictions.


SECTION 8 — GLOSSARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

AHJ — Authority Having Jurisdiction. The body that enforces code locally.
EDGE — Excellence in Design for Greater Efficiencies; IFC/World Bank green-building
       certification with a 20% resource-savings threshold.
FF&E — Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment.
MEP — Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing (building services).
MMDA — Metropolitan/Municipal/District Assembly; the local government body that
       issues building permits in Ghana.
O&M — Operations & Maintenance (manuals handed over at completion).
PPE — Personal Protective Equipment.
RFI — Request for Information (a contractor's query during construction).
RIBA — Royal Institute of British Architects; their Plan of Work is the basis for
       our work stages.
Snagging — Identifying defects to be fixed before/at handover.
VAT — Value Added Tax and associated statutory levies.
